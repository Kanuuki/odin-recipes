Orange Chicken

This is one of my all time favorite foods. If I could I'd live off of this for the rest of my life.

This food is most often known as a variation of General Tso's chicken in North America rather than in mainland China. The chef named Andy Kao claims to have developed the original Chinese-American oragne chicken recipe at a Panda Express in Hawai'i in 1987.

The list of ingredients' you'll need are shown below:

  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 2 tbsp orange juice
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1/3 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 1/2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp grated orange zest
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 tsp minced fresh ginger root
  • 1/2 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tbsp chopped green onion
  • 1/3 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp water

Combine these ingredients' in the order below:

Chicken:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs cut into 1/2 inch pieces
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper
  • 3 tbsp olive oil

Directions

  1. Step 1: Pour 1 1/2 cups water, orange juice, lemon just, rice vinegar and soy sauce. set over medium-high heat.
  2. Step 2: Stir in the orange zest, brown sugar, ginger, garlic, chopped onion and red pepper flakes. Bring to a boil, cool for 10-15 mins
  3. Step 3: Place chicken pieces into a resealable plastic bag. When sauce cooled, our 1 cup of sauce into bag. Remove remaining sauce. Seal bag, refrigerate at least 2 hours
  4. Step 3: In another bag, mix flour, salt and pepper. Add marinated chicken pieces, seal and shake
  5. Step 4: Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Place chicken into skillet and brown on both sides. Drain on a plate lined with paper towels
  6. Step 5: Wipe out the skillet, and add the sauce. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Mix together the cornstarch and 2 tbsp water; stir into the sauce. Reduce heat to medium-low, add the chien piece and simmer for about 5 minutes.
